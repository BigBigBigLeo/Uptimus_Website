# Uptimus - EV Charging Reliability Platform

## Project Introduction & Current Progress

Uptimus, previously referred to as Reaxio, is an AI-driven venture focused on improving EV charger reliability, repair speed, and operator uptime outcomes.

As EV adoption scales across Europe, charging uptime remains one of the biggest operational bottlenecks. Networks continue to expand, but repair workflows are still fragmented across OEMs, service contractors, field technicians, and operators. Mean time to repair is often measured in days rather than hours, while both users and operators increasingly expect infrastructure-grade reliability.

Uptimus is being built as an operating layer for EV charging reliability. The current product direction combines fault triage, repair orchestration, technician coordination, structured repair logging, and predictive maintenance preparation into one workflow.

## What Uptimus Does

Uptimus combines several core capabilities into a single platform:

- AI-assisted repair orchestration
  - turns charger faults into structured operational actions
- Intelligent dispatch workflow
  - helps match jobs to technicians based on skill, geography, and availability
- Real-time charger monitoring and ticketing
  - supports fault visibility and repair workflow tracking
- Structured repair logging
  - captures repeatable field data that can improve future diagnosis and planning
- Predictive maintenance preparation
  - uses telemetry, service logs, and repair workflows to support later predictive models

The ambition is to help operators move toward >99% uptime while bringing repair response and resolution times down materially.

The most relevant near-term funding path appears to be the MIT Noord-Holland feasibility track rather than jumping immediately into a larger R&D collaboration grant.

Why this matters:

- Uptimus is still validating scope, pilot shape, partner structure, and buyer path
- the feasibility route is aligned with answering whether the concept should move into deeper R&D
- a later MIT R&D collaboration path may become more suitable once stronger SME partners and pilot structure exist
